Days 1 – 7 · Week 1
Foundation & Paper
0 / 0

The legal and administrative scaffolding. Most of this is online and same-day. Get every item started on Day 1 in parallel — none of these steps depend on each other.

Entity & Tax Formation

Form a Florida LLC via Sunbiz.org ($125). Choose a name that is generic enough to survive a brand pivot — avoid locking yourself to a specific make, model, or neighborhood. Get your EIN from IRS.gov the same day (free, 10-minute online process). File the DR-1 with the Florida Department of Revenue to register for sales tax — you will collect 7.5% in Hillsborough County.

Banking & Credit

Open a dedicated business checking account at a dealer-friendly bank. Seacoast Bank, Regions, or a local credit union tend to be easier than the big 4 for a new dealership. Apply for a business credit card for recurring expenses (insurance, DMS, ad spend). Pull all 3 credit bureau reports and start disputing anything easy — your score directly affects floor plan terms.

Education & Association

Book the earliest available FIADA 8-hour pre-licensing course. FIADA (Florida Independent Automobile Dealers Association) runs them weekly in the Tampa area. Join FIADA as a member ($400/yr) — the lot referrals and lender relationships pay for themselves inside the first month.

Preliminary Lot Research

While paperwork is in motion, start your saved searches on LoopNet and Crexi for commercial parcels zoned CG, IG, or with prior auto-use designation. Target zip codes: 33578, 33584, 33610, 33619, 33635. Set alerts for anything under $4,000/mo with 8+ vehicle capacity.

Phase 1 Checklist
Exit CriteriaLLC formed. EIN in hand. Business bank account open. Sales tax registration pending. FIADA course booked. Credit reports pulled. Lot search underway.
2
Days 8 – 14 · Week 2
The Lot Hunt
0 / 0

This is where 90% of first-time dealers stall. The right lot makes everything downstream easy; the wrong lot burns the plan. Zoning is the hidden killer — a beautiful lot in the wrong zoning district is a dead lot.

Target Zones

Focus on unincorporated Hillsborough County rather than the City of Tampa proper — zoning is friendlier and rents are lower. Best hunting grounds:

  • Gibsonton — US-41 corridor, traditional small-dealer territory
  • Riverview & Brandon outskirts — growing traffic, mixed commercial
  • Plant City — cheaper rent, slightly farther from buyer pool
  • Adamo Drive (SR-60) corridor — legacy auto row
  • US-301 south of Brandon — high visibility, commercial zoning

Zoning Requirements

You need CG (Commercial General), IG (Industrial General), or a parcel with a verified prior auto-sales use. Parcels with an existing auto-sales history are gold — permits and approvals are already cleared. Pull a zoning verification letter from the Hillsborough County Development Services office before signing anything. Do not trust the listing agent's description.

FLHSMV Minimums for the Lot

  • Permanent enclosed office (not a travel trailer)
  • Display area clearly separated from public right-of-way
  • Permanent dealer sign meeting local signage ordinance
  • Operational business phone line at the address
  • Restroom access for customers
  • Minimum display capacity typical target: 8–15 vehicles

Lease Terms

Negotiate month-to-month or 6-month initial term with option to extend. Do not sign a 3-year lease on your first lot. You will learn more in the first 90 days than you expect, and you need the flexibility to relocate if the location underperforms or zoning surprises surface. Target rent: $1,500–$3,000/mo.

Phase 2 Checklist
CriticalIf prior use is anything other than auto sales and zoning is not CG/IG, assume "no" until the county confirms otherwise in writing. Every month spent fighting a rezoning battle is a month of rent + no license + no revenue.
Exit CriteriaLot identified. Zoning confirmed in writing. LOI accepted. Lease ready to sign pending final inspection-day prep.
3
Days 15 – 21 · Week 3
License Application & Insurance
0 / 0

With the lot identified, you can now submit a real application. Everything in this phase has to happen before FLHSMV inspects the lot in Phase 4–5.

Complete Coursework & Fingerprinting

Finish the 8-hour FIADA course if not already done. Keep the completion certificate — you will include it in the application packet. Schedule Livescan fingerprinting at an FDLE-approved vendor ($50–$80). Results go directly to FLHSMV.

Surety Bond

Florida requires a $25,000 surety bond for a VI (Independent) dealer license. With decent credit, the annual premium runs $250–$500. Use a surety specialist like Lance Surety, JW Surety Bonds, or Surety1. The bond goes in the name of the LLC, not the owner personally. Binding the bond takes 24–48 hours.

Garage Liability Insurance

Required by FLHSMV and every floor plan lender. Quote three carriers specifically writing Florida dealer policies: Federated Insurance, American Family, Kemper Specialty. Typical first-year premium: $1,500–$3,500. You can often pay monthly for an added fee. The binder letter is required for your application packet.

Lease Execution & Physical Setup Initiation

With zoning confirmed, sign the lease. Immediately order your permanent dealer sign (lead time is 1–2 weeks — don't wait). Establish a dedicated landline number at the address — FLHSMV still requires a working phone line at the physical dealership. VoIP options like Ooma or a basic Frontier business line work.

Submit HSMV Form 86056

The application itself is HSMV Form 86056. Walk it in to the FLHSMV regional office in Tampa rather than mailing it — faster processing, you can fix minor issues on the spot. Application fee is approximately $300. The packet includes: completed form, bond, insurance binder, certificate of completion, lease, fingerprint receipt, entity documents, sales tax certificate, and photos of the lot and office.

Phase 3 Checklist
Exit CriteriaApplication submitted. Bond and insurance active. Lease signed. Dealer sign on order. Landline active. Awaiting FLHSMV inspection scheduling.
4
Days 22 – 28 · Week 4
Lot Setup & Inspection Prep
0 / 0

Turn the empty space into a recognizably legitimate dealership. FLHSMV will inspect for basic commercial presence, not retail polish — but "basic" still has specific requirements.

Office Buildout

You need a real office with: a desk, two customer chairs, a locking filing cabinet for titles and contracts, a computer with a printer/scanner, and working phone. Total cost with used furniture: $1,500–$3,000. Do not overspend here — customers almost never care. The file cabinet is the one thing that must be high quality: you will store signed titles in it, and they cannot be lost or unsecured.

Signage & Display

Install the permanent dealer sign. Verify the install complies with the lease terms and local signage ordinance. Clean and organize the display area. Stripe parking spaces if needed. Clearly delineate the customer parking area from the inventory area.

Dealer Management System

Start your Frazer trial ($55/mo after trial). Frazer is the de facto standard for small independents — handles inventory, buyer's orders, titles, bill of sale, F&I math, and state reporting. Alternatives: DealerCenter, AutoManager, AutoRaptor. Begin learning the software now so you're fluent by the time your first car arrives.

Tag Agency Relationship

Identify 1–2 Tampa-area tag agencies that handle dealer title work. Introduce yourself in person. Ask about their dealer turnaround time and how they prefer paperwork submitted. Typical dealer-friendly agencies in the Tampa area handle title transfers in 3–7 business days.

Floor Plan Applications

With the license pending and lot ready, submit applications to NextGear Capital, Westlake Flooring, and Automotive Finance Corp (AFC) in parallel. Lenders will conditionally approve pending issuance of your license — they need to see the lot, your personal financial statement, business entity docs, and garage liability coverage. Applying to multiple lenders is standard practice and does not offend anyone.

Inspection Binder

Assemble every document FLHSMV might ask for into a single binder: application copy, bond, insurance, lease, course certificate, LLC docs, EIN letter, sales tax certificate, photos, sign installation receipt, phone bill. Walk the lot with the FLHSMV inspection checklist 48 hours before the inspection date.

Phase 4 Checklist
Exit CriteriaLot is inspection-ready. All three floor plan applications in underwriting. Frazer account set up. Tag agency relationships opened.

Days 29 – 35 · Week 5
License Issuance & Auction Access
0 / 0

The license unlocks everything downstream. Once you pass inspection, 3–10 days later you have plates, auction access, and a live floor plan.

FLHSMV Inspection Day

Be physically present. Bring the binder. Answer questions directly. If the inspector notes a correctable issue, fix it same-day and request a re-inspection. Most fails are for trivial things: missing a sign detail, phone not answering, office furniture not in place, or an unlocked title cabinet.

License Issuance

After passing inspection, the license is typically issued in 3–10 days. Once issued, display it prominently in the office — required by state law and expected by every auction and lender. Order 5 dealer plates through FLHSMV (~$50 each). You will need them for test drives, trade pickups, and auction-to-lot transport.

Finalizing the Floor Plan

With the license issued, your conditional floor plan approvals become real. Pick one primary lender. NextGear is the default recommendation for a first-time Tampa dealer — they are on-site at Manheim Tampa, their interface is the most forgiving, and dealer auction integration is seamless. Westlake is an excellent backup for units NextGear declines. Typical starting line: $75,000–$150,000.

Auction Access

Register at Manheim Tampa (5109 Cone Rd) and ADESA Tampa. Registration requires your license, a floor plan letter from your lender, and garage liability certificate. Once approved, install the Manheim Express app, set up your dealer profile, and get familiar with the bidding interface before your first sale day.

Days 36 – 42 · Week 6
First Inventory
0 / 0

Buy slow. Buy boring. The first 5 cars should be the three things Tampa buyers want most: reliable, affordable, and familiar. Avoid anything that looks interesting to you personally.

First Auction as Observer

Attend Manheim Tampa's Thursday sale without buying the first time. Watch how cars run through the lane, how fast the bidding moves, how the arbitration process works, and how dealers inspect units in the pre-sale lot. You will learn more in three hours of watching than in any training video.

Target Inventory

For a Tampa independent, the highest-velocity inventory is:

  • Price band: $8,000 – $15,000 retail
  • Makes: Honda, Toyota, Nissan, Hyundai, Kia
  • Body: Sedans and small SUVs (Civic, Corolla, CR-V, Rogue, Elantra, Sportage)
  • Mileage: Under 120,000
  • Condition: Clean Carfax, no accidents, no branded titles
  • Target gross: 20–25% after recon

Avoid: European luxury, anything with AC issues in Florida, lifted trucks, project cars, anything that "just needs a little work".

Buy & Recon Loop

For each unit you bid on, run the Carfax and AutoCheck before bidding. Inspect in person in the pre-sale lot. After purchase, budget:

  • $50–$100 pre-sale mechanical inspection
  • $150–$400 typical mechanical recon
  • $75–$150 detail
  • $50–$100 transport if needed

Build relationships with 1–2 local independent shops for reliable recon. Avoid chains for recon work — too slow and too expensive.

Photograph & Catalog

Each car gets 20+ photos minimum: exterior (8 angles), interior (dash, seats, rear seats, cargo), engine bay, underneath, tires, odometer, VIN, and any notable features. Add a 60-second walkaround video. Photography happens after detail, in bright natural light. Enter each into Frazer immediately with VIN, cost basis, recon cost, and target retail.

Phase 6 Checklist
Cash DisciplineWith a floor plan line of $100k, do not buy $100k worth of cars. Buy $40k–$60k for your first round. You want to prove turn velocity before stretching the line. Curtailments on unsold units will destroy your margin if you over-buy early.
Exit Criteria4–6 inspection-ready, detailed, photographed units on the lot. Each entered in Frazer with full cost and target pricing.
7
Days 43 – 49 · Week 7
Listings & Go-Live
0 / 0

Get the inventory in front of buyers. For Tampa independents, Facebook Marketplace is still the highest-ROI channel, followed by Craigslist and CarGurus. Paid listing aggregators are optional in month one.

Listing Channels

  • Facebook Marketplace — free, largest Tampa buyer pool, refresh every 48 hours
  • Craigslist Tampa Bay — free, still active, especially for $8k–$12k range
  • Offerup — free, growing, good for mobile-first buyers
  • CarGurus — paid ($300–$800/mo), optional month 1, recommended month 2
  • Cars.com / AutoTrader — paid, higher-intent buyers, recommended after the first 10 sales
  • Your own site — Frazer includes a basic site, or use Dealer Car Search ($100–$200/mo)

Google Business Profile

Set up the Google Business Profile listing for the dealership. Add 20+ photos of the lot, office, and key inventory. Request reviews from anyone who's bought a car informally from you, any early customers, friends, and family. A profile with 10–15 reviews in month one dramatically outperforms one with zero.

Website Launch

A simple one-page website is fine for month one. Must have: phone number, address with map, business hours, inventory feed from Frazer, "text us" button that goes to your dealer line. Do not over-engineer — 90% of leads in the first 90 days will come from marketplace listings, not your website.

Soft Open

Before paid ads, run a soft open: invite friends, family, FIADA contacts, and anyone you know who might be looking for a car. Get real foot traffic on the lot. Watch how they interact with the inventory. Take feedback on pricing and presentation. It is infinitely easier to adjust before you are paying for ads.

First Paid Ad

Launch a small Facebook ad campaign. $200 budget, 7-day run. Target: Tampa, Brandon, Riverview, Plant City; ages 25–55; interest category "buying a car" or similar. Creative: your best-looking unit on a bright background. Destination: direct message to your dealer line or a Facebook lead form.

Phase 7 Checklist
Exit CriteriaAll inventory listed across 3+ channels. Google Business Profile live. Website launched. First ads running. Lead response process in place.

Days 50 – 60 · Week 8–9
First Sales & Systems
0 / 0

Close the loop: first sale, first title filed, first curtailment paid, first lessons learned. Everything gets harder and easier at the same time.

Financing Partners

Not every buyer pays cash. Get approved with at least 3 lenders spanning the credit spectrum:

  • Credit Acceptance — subprime workhorse, will fund almost any credit profile
  • Westlake Financial — subprime / near-prime, separate division from their floor plan arm
  • Global Lending Services — subprime, active in Florida
  • Capital One Auto Navigator — prime, easiest prime lender for new dealers
  • Local credit unions (Suncoast, GTE Financial, MIDFLORIDA) — prime buyers, often excellent rates

Transaction Systems

Template every document in Frazer: buyer's order, bill of sale, odometer disclosure, privacy notice, as-is acknowledgment, Florida title application. Frazer generates these automatically once your dealer info is configured. Run through a full dry-run transaction (bill of sale through title submission) before your first real customer.

First Sales Execution

When the first real customer arrives, follow the process. Do not improvise. Complete the buyer's order in Frazer. Collect ID, insurance proof, and payment. Sign all required documents. File title paperwork through your tag agency within the state deadline (Florida dealer title transfers have specific timing requirements — confirm with your tag agency). Pay off the floor plan unit the same day you sell it.

Curtailment Reality Check

The first curtailment payment typically hits 45–60 days after purchase on each unit. If a car is still on the lot at day 45, the lender wants a partial principal payment (typically 10–15% of the original price). This is why the "buy slow" rule from Phase 6 matters. Track curtailment due dates per unit in Frazer or a spreadsheet — missing one is a covenant violation.

Early Metrics & Review

Track these weekly from day 50 forward:

  • Gross profit per unit (target $2,000+ avg)
  • Days on lot per unit (target < 30)
  • Cost per lead from each channel
  • Lead-to-showing conversion
  • Showing-to-sale conversion (target 30%+)
  • Total floor plan interest + curtailments paid

Risk Register

The three risks that kill first-time Tampa dealerships before month three. Mitigate them preemptively — they are all avoidable with proper diligence.

Zoning Surprise Critical · Phase 2

Lot is not actually zoned for auto sales, or prior-use designation was misrepresented. You discover this after signing the lease and spending weeks on setup. Result: lost rent, lost time, sometimes a legal battle.

MitigationNever sign a lease without a written zoning verification letter from Hillsborough County Development Services. Make zoning confirmation a contingency in the LOI. Walk away from any landlord who won't allow you to verify.

Floor Plan Denial High · Phase 4

Your primary floor plan lender declines. Usually driven by personal credit, cash reserves, or lack of dealer experience. Result: no line of credit, no inventory, license sits idle.

MitigationApply to NextGear, Westlake, and AFC in parallel in Phase 4. One will approve even if others decline. If all three decline, consider starting smaller with cash inventory only for 60–90 days until you have a sales track record to apply with.

FLHSMV Inspection Fail Medium · Phase 5

Inspector finds a correctable deficiency — missing sign details, phone not answering, office not furnished, title cabinet unlocked, or display area not meeting minimum. Adds 1–2 weeks to timeline.

MitigationPre-walk the FLHSMV inspection checklist 48 hours before inspection. Have every document in a binder. Test the phone line. Ensure sign, office, and display are all in place. Fix any issue on-site during inspection if possible.

Over-Buying Inventory High · Phase 6

You get approved for a $150k floor plan line and buy $150k of inventory immediately. By day 45, multiple units haven't sold, curtailments compound, interest accrues, and your cash reserves evaporate.

MitigationUse 40–50% of your floor plan line for the first round. Prove the sell-through cycle before stretching. Target: every unit out the door in under 30 days. Any unit approaching 45 days gets aggressive repricing.

Title Processing Delays Medium · Phase 8

Florida has strict timing requirements for dealer title transfers. Missing a deadline creates customer friction, tag agency complications, and possible state penalties.

MitigationBuild a tight relationship with 1 dealer-friendly tag agency. Confirm their dealer turnaround time in advance. Submit paperwork same-day or next-day after every sale. Keep titles in a locked, organized, single location.

Floor Plan Lender Directory

Active floor plan lenders serving the Tampa independent dealer market. Apply to at least two in parallel for redundancy.

NextGear Capital

Primary Recommendation

Manheim-owned. On-site representation at Manheim Tampa. Most forgiving of new dealer profiles. Industry-standard interface. Best choice for first-time Tampa independents.

Westlake Flooring

Strong Backup

Aggressive with new and subprime dealer profiles. Competitive rates. Will often approve lines that NextGear declines. Strong digital tools.

Automotive Finance Corp

Established Alternative

KAR-owned (parent of ADESA). Strong presence at ADESA Tampa. Slightly tighter underwriting than NextGear but competitive once approved.

Kinetic Advantage

Independent Focus

Newer, aggressive dealer-friendly programs. Good middle-ground option. Worth an application for rate comparison.

Floor Plan Xpress

Smaller Shop

Smaller lender with lighter underwriting. Good option if the larger lenders all decline. Higher rates but faster approval.

Advantage Funding

Specialty Lender

Auction-focused floor plan provider. Sometimes approves marginal credit profiles with larger down payment requirements.
